Audit Guide

Briefing Document: Q2 Shrink and Cash Loss Audit â€“ FY24/25

1. Introduction

This document summarizes the key changes, focus areas, and requirements for the upcoming Q2 Shrink and Cash Loss Audit. The audit period runs from Monday, January 27th to Friday, April 25th, with a brief pause over Easter (April 14th-21st). The core objective of the audit is to assess compliance with Loss Prevention (LP) standards and drive improvements in key areas, ultimately minimizing shrink and cash loss.

2. Key Changes and Focus Areas

The Q2 audit introduces some key changes and re-introduces some questions, demonstrating a refinement of focus areas. The pass rate remains at 85%, with all 45 questions being marked (no unmarked questions).

  • Removed Question: The question regarding recording loose produce waste has been removed.
  • Re-introduced Questions: Waste Management: The audit now assesses if the store has "a clearly identifiable waste area set up for Fresh foods / Frozen & Ambient areas" and whether a "clearly identified waste pre validation area set" is in place. Furthermore, it will be checked if "validated waste is stored separately as part of backhaul, tidy and ready to be returned on salvage." This shows an increased focus on proper waste handling and segregation. As the document explains, "Failure to store these separately may result in waste being missed through validation which increases the potential for unrecorded waste."
  • Food to Go Pizzas: The process of preparing "Food to Go pizzas" will be checked against the "correct specification."
  • Updated Questions: Product Protection (BWS): Compliance with new spirits caps and champagne nets will be checked in applicable stores.
  • Scorecard (Transfers): The "Cafe Transfers" metric is broadened to include all transfers, aligning with the LP Shrink Scorecard. The metric now assesses "transfers generally and not just Cafe transfers."
  • Shrink (Tobacco Backstock): Auditors will now verify that all tobacco and vape products are "stored securely" and will ask to see "any storage areas in the kiosk area". "It is important that the key processes are followed for tobacco to ensure all stock received is correctly stored once it has arrived at our stores to prevent shrink and maintain availability for our customers."

3. Audit Process and Structure

  • Duration: The audit is a 90-minute assessment.
  • Scoring: A pass requires a score of 85% or higher across the 45 marked questions.
  • Re-audits: "Selected stores may also receive a solo re-audit for either hygiene or shrink following their Q1 result and so may be audited twice within this quarter." These re-audits will be at least 4 weeks after the initial audit, and their scores will not be included in Regional Reporting, serving as a tool to drive improvement.
  • Action Plans: Following each audit, a list of non-conformances and required actions will be sent after the audit challenge window is closed. "Actions should be signed off by the Store Manager and should be closed out within one week."
  • Self-Assessment: Stores can access a self-assessment platform reflecting the Q2 changes and can complete this assessment using a Google Form. "The full self-assessment dashboard so that you can analyze your performance & track improvement can be found here". Prefilled information for the store can be used for this.

4. Key Audit Questions and Criteria

The audit covers a wide range of areas, including:

  • Scorecard Metrics: These are data-driven, taken from the two weeks preceding the audit, and include waste validation rates, outstanding transfers, dumpcodes, alarm settings, news & mags credit loss, product scan completion on news & mags, and smart gate utilization.
  • Online: 100% match between products in online totes/Amazon bags and the receipt.
  • Product Protection: Covering BWS, health & beauty, baby products, toys, small domestic appliances, batteries, printer ink, in-store produced beef, lamb & pork. The audit emphasizes, "...UP TO 3 UNIQUE PRODUCTS (IRRELEVANT OF QUANTITY) MAY BE UNPROTECTED BEFORE RESULTING IN A FAIL" in several of the product protection sections. The updated guidance will be checked for spirits caps and champagne nets. High-value stock must be secured in a high-value lock-up, cupboard, room, or cage. Nutmeg clothing also has to be correctly product protected.
  • Aisle Capping: The audit checks that high-value stock such as Health & Beauty products is not on aisle capping shelves.
  • Restricted Fill Indicators: Compliance with the "Restrict Fill" policy, including locked spirits doors where applicable.
  • Security: This section checks CCTV systems (cameras, monitors, guarding podiums), security guard uniforms and alertness, guard knowledge of top 5 loss lines, swing gate function, EAS barrier function and testing, smart gate function (where applicable), main bank till barriers, and front-facing self-checkout cameras. Also checks for working mag locks and external doors. Keys must be locked away and signed in/out, and the keyholder list and alarm codes must be up to date.
  • Shrink: Checks colleague practice on kiosk (handing over product before payment), weekly colleague searches, items found in store without receipt, kiosk door security, placement of carrier bags at SCOs, weekly shrink meetings, and the processing of single cans.
  • Unknown Loss: Checks correct preparation of cream cakes and food-to-go pizzas (weighing a sample for compliance), if the self-service area is manned and if colleagues are able to describe the use of the smart gate fob, if transfers into canteen and SRP break tables are being recorded, and if there is a clearly identified waste area.
  • Cash: Reviews if till/banking differences are investigated, if phased till checks are performed daily, if coupon bags are being returned correctly and if the cash logbook is completed.

5. Specific Question Examples

  • Unknown Loss 5 (Pizzas): "Weigh 10 in-store produced pizzas using the Food to Go scales. If the pizza weighs more than 10% over the specification it has failed. If 2 or more of these are overweight the question is failed."
  • Unknown Loss 6 (Waste): "Visually check there is a clearly identified waste pre-validation area set up... Visually check validated waste is stored separately as part of backhaul... Pass: There are 2 separate clearly identified waste areas for pre-validated waste and validated waste."
  • Shrink 4 (Tobacco): "The auditor will ask to see where tobacco and vape backstock is stored... Pass - Access cannot be gained into the kiosk - Bunker is locked where applicable - All tobacco & vape products are stored securely Fail - Tobacco and vape products are stored anywhere other than the following areas: - Tobacco Bunker... "
  • Protection 1 (BWS): "Visually check BWS products... Up to 5 units may be unprotected, or protected incorrectly before resulting in a fail."
  • Security 1 (CCTV): "Podium not working fully (no repair call logged) - CCTV podium obstructed by stock - Monitors not working / blank / hazy / switched off ..."

6. Key Resources and Contacts

  • Self Assessment Platform & Dashboard: Links to the self-assessment form, dashboard, and engagement pack are provided.
  • Prefilled Information: Prefilled store information is available for self-assessments.
  • Key Contacts: Terri Waters (Audit and Stocktake Manager), Hannah Cleary and David Grey (Senior Loss Prevention Managers), and Robert Palmer (Loss Prevention Specialist - Data & Analytics).
