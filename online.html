Trolley Setup (PDF p24): Prepare trolleys in advance using 'Prepare Trolley Application'. Scan trolley barcode, then scan each tote barcode into the correct position (A-H). Use Yellow totes for Ambient/Fresh, Grey for Frozen (check regime requirements).

Tote Hygiene & Washing (PDF p41, p100): 'Clean As You Go' for spills. Check totes are clean before pick walk. Follow scheduled weekly deep cleaning process with store cleaning team (hot water pressure wash). Store totes cleanly indoors. Use clear bags for raw meat/chemicals separation.

Tote Checking (PDF p104): Daily checks on buffered totes are crucial. Use HHT (OSP > Menu > Search Barcode > Scan Tote > Contents) or MySecurity App. Look for:

  • Missing items (marked picked but not present).
  • Additional items (not ordered/picked).
  • Damaged items ('Would I Buy It?' standard).
  • Date code issues (min 3 days shelf life usually required - check specifics).
  • Poor packing standards (heavy items at bottom, fragile on top).
Correct issues by adding/removing items and updating OSP, or substituting if needed. Log checks in MySecurity.

Use the Store Pick Progress Report daily to monitor picking efficiency and availability.

  • Access the report here (Example Link - Needs Verification).
  • Review Top Lines Non Picked to identify root causes (stock record, replenishment, range).
  • Use Store Mobile App / HHT to investigate stock record accuracy.
  • Monitor individual colleague performance (availability %, picks per hour). Target: 99% avail (inc subs), 96% (ex subs). Provide coaching/support where needed.
  • Feed back persistent issues during Huddles/Store Walks.

Service Standards & Escalation

Our 6 Online Customer Promises (PDF p5):

  1. Send ordered products or suitable alternative if unavailable.
  2. Allow customer choice on substitutes/missing items (inform them).
  3. Pick, handle, and pack items with care.
  4. Pick products with the best quality and date life.
  5. (Partners/C&C Focus): Provide timely and efficient handover/collection service.
  6. (C&C Focus): Happily assist with loading shopping into the customer's car if requested.

Quality Checks (PDF p97, 99): Always apply the 'Would I Buy It?' test. Check dates (min 3 days rule), packaging integrity (no dents/damage), freshness (no mould, wilting, bruising). Handle items carefully.

Bagless Operation: Most items are placed directly into totes.

Required Bags (Clear Bags - CAR95011): Use for raw meat/fish, chemical products (like bleach), unwrapped loose seeds, flowers/bulbs, soil-contaminated goods (potatoes/plants) to prevent cross-contamination (PDF p100).

Packing Order: Heavy/robust items at the bottom, lighter/fragile items on top. Keep chilled/frozen separate where tote types differ. Ensure totes aren't overfilled.

Picking Trolley Guidance (PDF p101): Keep trolley with you. Avoid picking from list for multiple items at once to prevent tote errors. Don't leave trolleys unattended.

Missing items (charged but not received) are a major complaint driver (PDF p102-103).

Causes & Prevention:

  • Picker Errors: Placing items in wrong tote (scan tote barcode *every time*), miscounting multiple items (double-check count), picking wrong variation (verify product details). Leaving trolley unattended.
  • Handover/Collection Errors: Ensure *all* totes are scanned and handed over/loaded. Perform adjustments on HHT *before* marking order complete if customer rejects items.

Manager Action: Perform regular Tote Checks. Coach pickers on careful checking and packing.

Applies to Alcohol and other age-restricted products (knives, etc.) for C&C and partner deliveries (PDF p21, 110).

  • If customer looks under 25, request valid photo ID (Passport, Driving Licence, PASS Card).
  • Verify age (must be 18+).
  • If valid ID shown and customer is 18+, complete handover. Record challenge on HHT if prompted/applicable.
  • If no valid ID / underage / showing signs of intoxication, refuse the age-restricted items. Remove items from order using HHT adjustment before completing.
  • For C&C, follow guidance on visual ID checks through car windows if customer is self-isolating (PDF p31).

Key principles for excellent C&C service (PDF p109-111):

  • Preparation: Check order for unavailable/substituted items before customer arrives.
  • Greeting: Warm, friendly welcome, thank for waiting.
  • Communication: Advise on any subs/shortages, allow customer to reject items.
  • Packing/Handover: Ask customer preference (unload for them vs self-pack). Offer Fresh Check. Load car carefully if requested.
  • Efficiency: Aim to meet wait time target (4m 15s). Use HHT sound alerts. Mark complete promptly.
  • Demeanour: Be natural, smile, engage positively.
  • Safety: Follow distancing/Challenge 25/Safety protocols.

Follow structured escalation for issues (PDF p47, 49, 51, 75, 79).

  • Initial Troubleshooting: Use guides, check connections, restart devices.
  • Store Buddy System: Contact neighbouring store manager/team for advice on operational queries (Dial 7170 + Store No + 269).
  • IT Service Desk (0345 611 6500): For technical faults (Devices, Network, OSP/Partner Platform Issues). State 'Online Home Delivery (Store Pick)'. Note Priority Levels (P1=Major Outage, P2=Multiple Stores/Part Delivery Affected, P3=Single Device/Minor Issue). Get ticket reference.
  • Partner Support: Use direct support channels for Amazon/Deliveroo etc. for platform-specific issues if IT Desk cannot resolve.
  • Regional Operations Manager: Escalate unresolved IT issues (provide ticket ref), major incidents (accidents, security), persistent operational problems, route cancellations (rarely applicable for C&C/Partner).
  • Specific Issues: Use dedicated processes for Road Traffic Accidents (PDF p52), Property Damage (PDF p70 - LIABREP), Security Incidents (PDF p55 - MySecurity), Lost Keys/Equipment (PDF p10, 60).
